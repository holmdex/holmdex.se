Understanding Market Charts

Charts display the price movement of an asset over time. The green and red candlesticks indicate whether the price increased (green) or decreased (red) during that time period.

Each candlestick represents a specific time period and shows four key price points:

  • Open: The price at the beginning of the time period
  • Close: The price at the end of the time period
  • High: The highest price reached during the time period
  • Low: The lowest price reached during the time period

Technical indicators can be added to analyze patterns and predict future price movements based on historical data.

Understanding Forex Pairs

Currency pairs show the exchange rate between two currencies. The first currency (base) is being valued in terms of the second currency (quote).

For example, in EUR/USD:

  • EUR is the base currency
  • USD is the quote currency
  • A rate of 1.10 means 1 Euro is worth 1.10 US Dollars

Forex markets operate 24 hours a day, five days a week, with trading volume and liquidity varying throughout global trading sessions.

Understanding Commodities

Commodities are raw materials used in commerce and traded on specialized exchanges. They often serve as inflation hedges or diversification tools.

Major commodity categories include:

  • Precious Metals: Gold, Silver, Platinum, Palladium
  • Energy: Crude Oil, Natural Gas, Gasoline
  • Agricultural: Wheat, Corn, Soybeans, Coffee
  • Industrial Metals: Copper, Aluminum, Steel, Zinc

Commodity prices are influenced by supply and demand factors, geopolitical events, weather patterns, and currency movements.
