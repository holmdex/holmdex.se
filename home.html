Investment Fundamentals

Simple concepts to help you understand investing better

Diversification

Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Spread your investments to reduce risk.

Don't Put All Your Eggs in One Basket

Diversification is simply spreading your money across different investments. If one investment performs poorly, others might do well, helping to balance out your overall performance.

Mixing different types of investments reduces risk
Helps protect your money during market ups and downs
Think of it like having backup plans for your money

Risk Management

Understanding the balance between potential returns and risk levels.

Balancing Risk and Reward

Every investment carries some risk. Generally, investments with higher potential returns come with higher risks. Understanding how much risk you can handle is key to successful investing.

Higher potential returns usually mean higher risk
Your comfort with risk changes as you get older
There's no need to take on more risk than you're comfortable with

Time Horizon

The longer your investment timeframe, the more growth potential you have.

Time Is Your Friend

How long you plan to keep your money invested makes a big difference. The longer you can leave your money to grow, the more time it has to recover from temporary market downturns.

Starting early gives your money more time to grow
Long-term investing helps ride out market ups and downs
Money needed soon should be in safer investments

Cost Awareness

Small fees can significantly impact your investment returns over time.

Watch Those Fees

Fees might seem small, but they add up over time and can significantly reduce your returns. Being aware of all costs associated with your investments is important for maximizing what you earn.

Even small fees can take a big bite out of your returns
Low-cost index funds often perform better than expensive managed funds
Always ask about fees before investing
